After a brief confusion, the children regained their rhythm, chanting in unison for an encore until drums sounded, and the curtains lifted, welcoming the return of the hero.

The young Encore aspired to be a princess, longing for elegant dresses, a majestic crown, and a sumptuous bed to rest on. Free-spirited children like her didn't fret over monsters beyond the castle; that was the black Wooly's dutyoutside the castle was a knight's battlefield.
During those days, Cosmos and Cloudy were mere dolls, intimate friends of Encore. However, after playtime, they would always maintain a quiet distance, lying still in a box without uttering a word. It was only when the night fell that faint whispers emerged beside Encore's bed.
Their fluffy little hands would tenderly stroke Encore's cheeks, offering comfort, and pulling at her clothes to free her from the clutches of nightmares.
Don't be afraid, Encore; sweet dreams await you. Sleep well, for the adventures with the Woolies will be carefully preserved in dreams. A Heroine's Return
The first time Cosmos and Cloudy spoke to Encore was at her mother's funeral. It happened suddenly, and Encore can now still vividly recall watching the dirty soil and dried leaves falling onto her mother's coffin.
Initially, the dolls were nestled quietly in Encore's arms, as if they were lying in the box her mother carried for performances. However, as the funeral attendees began shoveling soil onto the coffin, Cloudy emitted an almost inaudible breath. The small episode went unnoticed by everyone except Encore.
"Encore, Encore..." the dolls gradually spoke up when they realized no one else was paying attention. Encore glanced around warily, ensuring no one else heard their voices.
"Let's go, Encore. I'm sooo bored," Cosmos bluntly complained. Surprisingly, its voice resembled Encore's more than her mother's.
"But... but Cloudy doesn't want to leave..." Cloudy whimpered. Encore struggled to discern whose voice it resembledsometimes like hers and sometimes like her mother's.
Then, a brief silence followed. The cold morning wind howled through the cemetery, across the mourning crowd, carrying the sound of soil falling onto the coffin further away.
"No! We can't wait! The show's about to end!" Cosmos urged.
As if remembering something, Cloudy became anxious, "Encore, Encore! Go to the theater! Let's go to the theater!"
Gazing into the distance, beyond the shadowy trees of the cemetery, Encore fixated on the theater's worn but still eye-catching sign. She heard the laughter of childrentheir uninhibited joy contrasting with the somber sounds of gravel and dirt.
Amidst the astonished and disdainful gazes of Encore's relatives, she rushed out of the cemetery, darting through alleys, and finally arrived at the abandoned theater. Encore pushed open the door, disrupting the lonely dust within.
Warm spotlights cast an unreal halo over the stage, illuminating the vast sea of flowers on the backdrop. Her mother's figure was blurry yet brilliantit was the mother Encore had been missing, not the withered body in the coffin.
"Mom..." tears shimmered in Encore's eyes as she stumbled toward the stage. But just as she was about to touch her mother, she was forcefully pulled into darkness.
The show concluded, lights dimmed, and the curtains tightly closed. Sadness, fear, and the things lurking in the darkest corners quietly infiltrated the theater. Encore stood helplesslyshe knew she was once again trapped in her nightmare.
"Encore!" Cosmos shouted.
"Encore!" Cloudy echoed.
To dispel Encore's fear and help her regain courage, Cosmos and Cloudy continued to shout out that very word that could let the hero return"encore". They persisted until Encore regained her senses and joined them in shouting her unique name"Encore".
The drums sounded, the curtains lifted, and the lights once again conquered the darkness. Amidst the applause of Cosmos and Cloudy, Encore stepped onto the stage as the new protagonist.
Overwhelmed, Encore couldn't help but burst into tears. All the sadness and fear were washed away with her streaming tears. Soon, her usual smile reappeared on her face. Now, it was Encore's turn to play the brave black Wooly knight. A new adventure was about to unfold.
This time, the story would undoubtedly have a happy ending. Orphanage and Candy House
Even as the Consultant of the Black Shores, Encore refuses to act like a grown-up. To her, growing up means bidding farewell to bedtime stories, a prospect she firmly rejects. The very idea of drifting into sleep with empty, dark dreams instead of the vibrant hues of wonderful tales doesn't sit well with her.
Gratefully, the adults in the Black Shores indulge her in maintaining her childlike demeanor. However, there lies a challengewho among the busy grown-ups can spare the time to read Encore a brief bedtime story? That's when Cloudy raises its little fluffy hand, promptly followed by the dark, fluffy hand of Cosmos.
On this particular evening, Cloudy brought Encore the old story of the Candy House.
"Then... then..." Cloudy's voice trailed off, growing softer. Oh no, it seemed to have forgotten the next part. Nervously, Cloudy glanced at its companion, lifting the covers and attempting to hide itself from the intense embarrassment.
"Let me! Let me!" Cosmos pushed Cloudy aside, standing beside Encore's bed, proudly taking over the storytelling. "Then, Lord Encore, blessed by blazing flames, defeated the big bad cat! Its dirty fur was all burnt by Lord Encore!"
"And what about Lord Encore's friends?"
"Everyone is safe because of Lord Encore. From then on, people live in happiness without worrying about the big bad cat!"
Encore immersed herself in the story until her eyelids succumbed to the irresistible call of sleep. She wanted to challenge the conclusion that "Director Mama was the big bad cat," but her drowsiness intervened. In the end, Cloudy and Cosmos, confused by Encore's half-formed objection, joined her under the soft covers, and they all drifted into a deep sleep.
In her dream, Encore found herself back at the orphanage, standing before the Candy House devoured by scorching flames.
The roof helplessly melted in the raging fire, sticky syrup dripping onto the big cat, further tormenting its weakening body. Huge but ugly, it's a blessing of sin. Now, it received another blessing of sindeath.
However, the Reaper refused to easily forgive this sinful soul soaked in innocent blood, choosing instead to torture it and revel in its reluctant confession.
Agonizing screams reverberated through the forest. All creatures, once aggressive hyenas and arrogant crows, cowered in fear. In this silent place, only Encore and the dying soul endured.
"It must be painful." The warm blaze cast a glow on Encore's face as she approached the dying cat. She gently pressed her cheek against its body, using her scarred little hand to stroke its fur.
The big cat resented Encore for thwarting its plan, expecting her to harbor resentment in return. After all, its evil deeds were cruel enough to earn the resentment of everyone. But why? Why did it feel nothing but warmth and sorrow from Encore's little hand?
The frenzied flames danced around the Candy House, seemingly intent on devouring everything. Yet, Encore was not afraid. She leaned against the big cat and started singing a lullaby.
It's a melody the big cat once heard when it was called "Director Mama" by Encore and other children in the orphanage. It had sung this song for each child.
Encore kept on singing, her voice becoming hoarse, until the blazing flames extinguished, until the first morning light graced the sky. She felt the warm sunlight on her fingertips, and the cat's "blessing" faded away, disappearing into the misty depths of the forest.
Looking down, the once huge, ugly cat had transformed, curling up in Encore's arms, sleeping soundly.
"Mama isn't the big bad cat!" Encore's murmurs disrupted the passing Black Shores night patrol and startled her. "Encore drove away the villain, so Mama no longer needs to be the big bad cat!" The night patrol shook her head in resignation, amused by the girl's unruly sleeping posture. She gently tucked Encore in and continued her patrol. A New Story, A New Adventure

The sight of someone donning sunglasses indoors intrigued her, but wearing them in a dimly lit place also struck her as peculiar. Encore couldn't quite figure out the reasoning behind it.
Upon noticing the nurse guiding this intriguingly quirky grown-up inside, Encore hastily returned to her bed, pretending to read her storybook.
"So... you're that kid from the news? The one from the orphanage... Oops, I should've known your name." Aalto removed his sunglasses, scrutinizing Encore with care. The idea of such a small child achieving something noteworthy seemed implausible to him. He recalled the headline he had glimpsed in the newspaper"Nine-Year-Old Girl Cracks Sensational Case". While uncertain about the reliability of tabloid content, he had a mission here. Even that person said, "This is Tethys' choice," and the deductions of the Tethys System could never go wrong.
"This uncle is strange. Encore can't understand what you're talking about." Setting aside her storybook, Encore studied Aalto, mirroring his earlier scrutiny. She speculated he might be a certain large dog, probably a wolfan affable gray wolf that wouldn't bare its fangs at her.
"Oh yeah, Encore. That's right. You're the one I'm looking for. Wait! How could you call me Uncle? Take a closer look. Do I look that old?" Aalto's distressed expression triggered laughter from Encore. Perhaps her hospital stay had left her a bit bored, so she decided to tease this quirky grown-up a little bit.
"Let Encore take a closer look... Encore's right! You're an uncle!"
Aalto sighed, "Well..." before continuing, "C'mon, say something nice. It's not a walk in the park to do this business. Besides, I've got to take care of the other little ones from the orphanage as well."
"Anyway, back to the point. Encore, do you want to come with me?" Aalto's tone shifted from playful to serious, but soon reverted to his usual light-heartedness. "You can have as many candies as you want. Well... not too many. It's bad for your teeth."
"That sounds suspicious," Encore replied, her bright eyes blinking with curiosity. Rather than answering Aalto's question, she picked up Cosmos and Cloudy, bringing them close to her ears. "Woolies? "Hey, I'll forgive you for calling me Uncle, but what's with the 'surprising'? Do I not seem like a stand-up guy at first glance?"
"Since Cosmos and Cloudy said you're a good person, Encore will go with you," she said, attempting to let herself sound formal, "but Cosmos and Cloudy will come too. They are my best friends."
"Sure," Aalto agreed without hesitation. After all, they were just two ordinary dolls. It somewhat caught his attention that Encore genuinely considered them as something alive.
It was an adventure, a new adventure! Encore joyfully spun around holding her friends, first with Cosmos, and then with Cloudy. She had grown a bit and now understood the importance of treating her friends fairly.
Suddenly, a crucial question struck Encore. "What are we going to do?"
