Evaluation Basis: [Resonance Assessment 2190-G]
Resonator Baizhi experienced her Awakening four years ago, after being exposed to a strong Remnant Energy source one year prior. This exposure caused the activation of the corresponding Remnant frequencies within her. According to Baizhi's recounts, she experienced a strong urge to revive a teammate during her Awakening. As a result, the aforementioned frequencies manifested as a Remnant Creature that now serves as her companion.
Resonator Baizhi's Tacet Mark is located on the anterior side of her right thigh. No significant physical alterations after Awakening have been observed. However, when exerting her Forte, the Tacet Mark emits a faint white glow with the advent of the above Remnant Creature. The creature is translucent white in color and resembles the petals of Epiphyllum. It is unable to vocalize in human language, but can communicate with Baizhi through telepathy.
Resonator Baizhi cannot directly utilize her Forte, but she can leverage the extremely low temperature and her Remnant Creature's healing abilities to treat injuries in her vicinity.
Resonator Baizhi's Resonance Spectrum Pattern does not resemble any known frequencies. However, it shows a certain level of similarity with the fluctuations of Remnant Energy, and it possesses a strong Syntony Response with "You'tan", her Remnant Creature. Despite these connections, the cause of Baizhi's Awakening remains unclear without adequate theoretical evidence.
The non-convergent Rabelle's Curve of Resonator Baizhi displays a gradual increase in the initial stage, followed by a sharp climb in the middle section. The test results thus categorize Baizhi as a Mutant Resonator with a Dormant Period.
The waveform graph of Baizhi illustrates an elliptical fluctuation with a stable Time Domain. No abnormal parameters are observed in the test sample.
Resonant Criticality: High. Resonator Baizhi's frequency displays high stability with currently no risk of Overclocking.
Baizhi has no documented history of Overclocking which reduces the necessity for counseling. Due to the strong bond between Baizhi and her Remnant Creature, regular examinations on the Remnant Creature's status are recommended to mitigate any potential latent risks. Principle of Simplicity
When mentioning Baizhi, her colleagues in the Academy would often remark that she is a hard person to approach.

Even though they work in the same department, her colleagues rarely see her as a conversation starter. On top of that, whether it is them exchanging frustrations about the failure of research, or the exchange of interesting stories during break time. Whenever their gazes fall on Baizhi, she is always seen being calm and unruffled, analyzing the data at hand in an orderly manner, like a module immersed in its own processing logic.

With Baizhi as the center point, silence spreads without a trace, slowly pulling her away from the rest of the crowd.

This is consistent with Baizhi being organized whilst in her research.

On Mondays and Wednesdays, Baizhi checks on the artificial Sonoro analyzes the Remnant frequency band on Fridays, and leaves the rest for field research, Remnant biology observation, and case integration. Casually making arrangements if necessary. She has composed a rigorous plan of her own for what to do in each stage, and when and where to appear. She follows her plan accordingly, making adjustments on the go when needed, and moving forward step by step.

If there's unfinished work, she does not fret, as she simply continues to work on it.
If there are problems that are difficult to solve, it bothers her little, as she proceeds to study further until gaining a firm grasp of them.

This sense of composure and self-sufficiency makes it difficult for others to find an opportunity to approach Baizhi. Her cold expression and objective and distant tone of voice when talking also make people involuntarily imagine further distancing scenes.

"Your report for the previous week was not submitted within its designated timeframe."

"Ah... Yes... I, uh... The original Remnant samples were contaminated. I had to..."
"Uhh, I'm sorry! No, I'm deeply sorry for my negligence! I... I'll definitely make up for..."
Seeing Baizhi approaching closer and closer because of his words, the reporting researcher nervously clutched his clothes and subconsciously lowered his head even lower, but the next moment, a processed sample slice was handed to him.
"I presume samples from a Fission Junrock would suffice?"
"But, that's your..."
"You may complete your report before the stasis-evolution. Having anticipated this potential scenario, I have allocated adequate time to mitigate any deviation."
"Please inform me ahead of time should any analogous scenarios occur in the future. You were... trembling intensively just now. May I suggest increasing the ambient temperature?"
A pair of azure eyes rarely show ripples, revealing a pure and unconcealed sense of confusion.
The researcher was stunned, then suddenly remembered the other half of the sentence about Baizhi that the other colleagues had added — Yet, it only appeared that way. The Sphere and the Device
A small sphere begins to roll, as it falls down a curve, the end of the lever tilted upwards. The transmission of force.
Eleven-year-old Baizhi stared at the sphere that had once again stopped at the pre-determined location and came to a conclusion: No matter how complicated it may look, the sphere would always follow the device's design and roll uncontrollably to its destined end. No exception.
This reminded her of a theoretical hypothesis she had read from a book not long ago: No matter which dynasty or which generation. The civilizations created by humans failed to break through the bottleneck of the apparatus and would eventually reach their destined downfall.
If the results are always consistent, then isn't everything that has been done just a repetition of the process? Is there truly a meaning to it?
As young Baizhi was troubled by the questions that derived from the sphere. Her parents were also equally troubled about their daughter.
Passion for knowledge is a feat worthy of praise, but it is obvious that Baizhi is overly indulged in scientific theories rather than in real life. She always buried herself in the study, reading obscure books that one may even find hard to name or using sample materials to simulate information bands. Just sitting here for a whole day, with almost no "living" friends.
It was until that afternoon, Baizhi received an invite to the scientific expedition base under the Huaxu Academy. And witnessed a device, greater in both size and complexity than any she had seen before——
Components symbolizing different variables were featured in it, convoluted yet accurately connected, as if simulating a type of organic system.
However, the deficiencies of this device cannot be ignored. Too many steps remain to be completed, and the uncertainty caused by possibilities renders it unsuitable to carry the sphere.
With pondering thoughts, Baizhi focused her gaze on the existing parts, developing hypotheses and various simulations.
"Have you taken an interest in it? Our team plans to finish it someday."
A leader-figure young man who had just returned from a scientific expedition smiled at Baizhi.
"Right there... If designed in such a way, the remaining energy will not be adequate to support the further distances, thus the information cannot be transmitted."
"Aah. So you've spotted its problem so swiftly... The Director was right about you. You really are a talented young lady! What say you? Do you wish to join us?"
"The sphere has only one end, which is to ultimately stop at a certain location. Therefore, I fail to see the true endpoint in your design."
"I would say, a place... unknown yet comprehensible? I agree an ending must be set. As to where and how the sphere should end up, being collective creators of this device, we can find an answer. Together."
As soon as the voice fell, the half-closed door slammed open, and the rest of the members of the team who had been hiding behind the door fell into the room, faces first. They looked at Baizhi with embarrassed and friendly faces, anticipating her response to join the team.
At that moment, Baizhi seemed to hear the slight crackling sound of the sphere being put into the very beginning point of the device Close Encounter
It was the fourth year after she'd joined the scientific expedition team when Baizhi first came into contact with the Remnant.
At that time, Baizhi's enthusiasm for information research was at its peak. She no longer immersed herself in a study, instead, she had the entire scientific expedition team at her side, diving into every nook and cranny of civilization.
She began to understand that true cold cannot be described with a simple phrase of "below 25°C", but rather depicted by endless white snow, by trembling even when equipped with weather protection gear, and the knocking of survey instruments before using otherwise they will freeze and become inoperable. The theory is just a skeleton, and only by combining existing knowledge with real feelings can it be considered complete.

Baizhi followed her team to various places. The target was not ordinary ruins, but a surreal space composed of Remnant energy. Sonoro. They believe that "Remnant" is pure and complete information energy, but it is invisible and intangible, they cannot capture it, let alone sample and analyze it.

A breakthrough came from the ninth Sonoro that Baizhi entered.
It was not a distorted and twisted image of erosion, nor was it a true reflection of an existing moment.
The array of buildings could not tell what material they were made of. The light was seen pouring down from the hole in the dome, giving them a layer of flowing pearl luster.
Baizhi and her team's arrival seemed to have triggered a mechanism. The buildings, originally suspended, began to change in their arrangement rapidly, forming unique images of symbols and numbers.
Baizhi couldn't make a judgment. She couldn't tell what information this Sonoro was conveying, just as she couldn't differentiate whether this scene she was witnessing was casting the past, present, or a future yet unknown. But there was no time for astonishment. The instinct of researchers drove everyone, including Baizhi, to race against time and record everything that was occurring. Perhaps all of this pointed to the possibility of a new theory——
The survey instruments displayed live fluctuation of frequencies. Baizhi cut off all distractions and focused on the displays. Her cumulated theories and feelings all came in handy for this exact moment.
The shorter the distance, the lower the energy consumption. Two points form a straight line, which leads to the source.
Just like constructing a device channel, Baizhi selected the two ends of the extension connected them into a line, and picked up the sampling device towards the center of the gathering—
The surging light responded to Baizhi's actions, condensing into a ball of pure white Spectrum.
The Remnant band was thus captured and manifested, full but like an empty shell, pure and free of impurities.
Baizhi didn't expect at that time that it would accompany her in another form and become the subject of her research throughout her life. Verging Towards Zero
For Baizhi, making a wish was a perplexing action.
"Knowing that wishes are unlikely to come true, what is the point of making them? This action does not even have a clear object. Once you make a wish, will it be answered? Who will answer it?" Faced with Baizhi's sincere questions, her teammates were once again left deeply perplexed. Fortunately, after being acquainted for more than a thousand days, they had already learned her seriousness of always wanting to be logical and understood that her complicated and indirect tone when judging or stating things was a way to get as close to the truth as possible and avoid inconsistencies.
Thus, though Baizhi never made a wish, she always stood beside her teammates when made wishes on candle lights, shooting stars, or anything else.
Just as they did for her, though confused, she still tried to understand—
"I hope we can completely unravel the mystery of Remnant!"
"It can be done, but unlikely within our generation."
"Ahh, it's true... yet disheartening nonetheless! No need to overthink it, making wishes and reality are two different things... Anyway, you'll understand when you have one!"

Under the dead silence of the ice field, the day finally came.
The collapse of the world often dawns without warning. Vivid figures are dismantled by Tacet Discords, and the very eyes that spoke of the days to come are dimmed and gradually grow cold. Baizhi once again felt the loss of pulse, the pounding of her own heart, and uncontrollable thoughts kept screaming inside her mind. Why, is this happening, so much has already been lost in finding the exact same Sonoro. No, it shouldn't be like this... She couldn't think of a future without them.
"I hope everyone would wake up."
She finally had a wish, but she didn't know who to make it to, nor did she know who could grant her her wish.
The Sonoro twisted and collapsed, and more and more Tacet Discords approached her. Hatred, sadness, unwillingness... Mixed emotions twisted inside her, but could not hamper the increasingly strong desire in her heart. She wanted everyone to wake up, open their eyes like before, and come back to her—
A Tacet Mark has been formed, and an Remnant creature taking the form of a Queen of the Night responded to Baizhi's wish and descended before her.
Nowadays, Baizhi still won't make wishes.
Wishes are not facts. She can only grasp those that she is capable of.

As for the sole wish she has ever made, it was like a number infinitely close to zero, hidden inside Baizhi's heart, impossible to comprehend. Yet it will not disappear. The Sphere Will Fall
Everything that happened under the glacier has become a thing of the past.
As the only survivor, Baizhi never mentions it on her own accord.
Those nosy who tried to find out her secrets, but they all came back empty-handed. There was no shady secret that could not be revealed. Everyone did their best. Except for the coincidence of luck, the public could not find any person to blame. Furthermore, to fall on the way of exploration is an ending that every researcher who embarks on the road of scientific expedition has imagined. They go to the extreme and use their bodies to measure the truth, but they all know that death is also a part of the truth.
Baizhi's silence is rather more of her not knowing how to convey her words.
Mentioning the event afterward won't change anything. Compared to words, Baizhi is more willing to spend time on more important things - the things that only she can do ever since she became the only one remaining.
The researcher who had just joined the Academy was seen rushing between various departments of the Remnant Ecoacoustics, making countless innovations in the fields of Waveband analysis, Remnant applications, and Sonoro simulations.

She proposed that Remnant creatures are energy bodies derived from "empty shells". They may be able to resonate with humans and be formed in a form beyond the band. By mastering the principle, humans may be able to develop artificial Sonoros and more Remnant creatures. These hypotheses shocked the academic community, but the Remnant creature that returned from Sonora with her was the best example.
She also named the Remnant creature, You'tan, corresponding to Utpala, a flower that blooms at night and withers the following morning.
The Dharma and ultimate truth are as elusive as the Epiphyllum blossom, both difficult to find in this world. However, all researchers have been searching for their entire lives, and what they seek is nothing more than this.
This name may be a commemoration of the fallen, or it may be a reminder to the namer herself to keep going and not stop. There are too many answers waiting to be found.
But Baizhi is not in a hurry. The destination must be somewhere. Before the sphere falls, she just needs to patiently walk every step as she progresses.
What's more, the path she chose is not a path of walking alone. In the process of searching, Baizhi once again met someone who could be called a partner.

In the outskirts of Huanglong, the distinguished guest did not know where he came from, but the moment Baizhi witnessed the other party absorbing the "sound", the invisible sound seemed to reappear in Baizhi's ears.
Just like the crackling sounds of the sphere falling into the device at the start—
Look, that unknown but knowable point can be found by humans. Simplistic Rings
Baizhi's worn pair of rings from her time at Huaxu Academy are identical in shape and style, with only slight differences in wear.
