Evaluation Basis: [Resonance Assessment 1888-G]
Resonator Lingyang's age and time of Awakening remain uncertain, and he has a long history of Awakening. Lingyang has endured prolonged exposure to intensely cold wilderness conditions and might have naturally awakened his Forte during confrontational battle.
Lingyang bears a Tacet Mark on his right arm. There is no sign of mutation on his body after Awakening.
Lingyang exhibits exceptional physical endurance and strength, due to his exposure to diverse extreme environments. He demonstrates proficiency in combat and survival with outstanding skills and agility. Lingyang shows no habitual use of Forte and in consequence lacks experience in employing Forte. He relies mostly on his physical strength in battle. An assessment is needed to determine whether Lingyang requires proper guidance in Forte usage.
Under the influence of Lingyang's Forte, his senses become enhanced. During battle, subject possesses the ability to generate an invisible freezing aura and a low-temperature field within a certain radius, exerting intimidating pressure on nearby enemies. Additionally, Lingyang can transmit his will to others within the field, influencing and motivating the emotions of those nearby.
Currently, no instances of Resonance Spectrum Patterns similar to that of Lingyang have been encountered. The cause of his Awakening remains undetermined.
The Rabelle's Waveform shows no convergence, exhibiting a slow upward trend. Consequently, Resonator Lingyang has been identified as a Natural Resonator.

"What? So his 'beast-like' appearance wasn't caused by Forte? What exactly is he..."
Resonator Lingyang's waveform graph shows elliptical fluctuations. The pattern of Time Domain is regular with stable peak values, and no abnormal waveforms are observed.
Resonant Criticality: Very stable. Resonator Lingyang possesses a rather high Criticality level. The risk of Overclocking is very low.
Lingyang has no past record of Overclocking.
Through testing, it has been determined that Lingyang demonstrates good control over his Forte, maintaining stable emotional state. As a result, when he uses his Forte, the "Will Transmission" within the low-temperature field generated by his Forte primarily manifests as "motivation" for Lingyang's allies. During group actions, the overall Overclocking risk for Lingyang is futher reduced.
It is recommended to maintain Lingyang's risk assessment level as low and perform routine observation. Auspicious Liondancer
The liondance in Jinzhou boasts a broad audience base. Whether it's grand events like freshman initiations and opening ceremonies or intimate occasions like brithday celebrations for the elderly and 100-day festivities for babies, there are always moments that call for a ritual and that's why the Liondance Troupe consistently attract a steady stream of clients.
From the moment Lingyang became a "Liondancer", Lingyang's role extends beyond the thrilling and somewhat risky performances on the Plum Blossom Poles. Crafting unique liondance routines for each client becomes an integral part of his responsibilities, ensuring that every performance leaves a lasting impression on his clients.
Lingyang enjoys engaging in communications with clients. On one hand, delving into the customer's needs is essential for tailoring a liondance that resonates with the client. On the other hand, it's purely a personal preference. To him, every individual's story is an undiscovered treasure, and he takes pleasure in unraveling these narratives. Many clients find it difficult to disappoint those curious and inquisitive eyes, so they always end up revealing their most genuine thoughts. This curiosity, though genuine, sometimes leaves his clients wondering if they have come to the wrong place, or if the Liondance Troupe has started a new business—could it be a "psychological counselion" perhaps?
The Liondance Troupe operates in a world of tight schedules, last-minute changes, and unforeseen emergencies. Managing conflicts and friction is a constant challenge, as a misstep could mean losing valuable clients. Therefore,understanding and responding to each client's emotions is the key to the Troupe's success. Excellent negotiation skills are paramount, and for Lingyang, honing these skills is just another conquest.
Luckily, Lingyang finds another way to deal with difficult clients—by giving lucky greetings. Armed with a bright smile and sincere well-wishes, Lingyang has the ability to make even the most unreasonable clients concede a bit. Gradually, his Lucky Greetings Collection has become essential reading for fellow Liondancers, turning "giving lucky greetings to everyone" into a signature feature of the troupe.
"Giving lucky greetings upon meeting someone. You must be from the Liondance Troupe, right? Please come in!"
However, Lingyang's lucky greetings aren't one-size-fits-all. In his opinion, the essence of giving lucky greetings lies in sincerity and understanding, ensuring each greeting is tailored to the recipient. Despite evolving into a formality for the Liondance Troupe, Lingyang remains committed to offering carefully thought-out and personalized blessings. Moreover, Lingyang genuinely believes "lucky greetings" do bring good fortune to people.
Therefore, if you come across a Liondancer showering you with lucky greetings, don't be skeptical. Just accept his pure and sincere blessings, for it's the best response to a kind and friendly soul. Fate
When delving into Lingyang's connection with the Liondance Troupe, we have to go back to that "ordinary" day when a liondance performance at the Jinzhou Theater had just concluded. As the staff was tidying up the stage props, a strange-looking youth dropped from the roof. To be more precise, it wasn't a mere "drop"; it's more like a "crash".
As it turned out, this was a carefully orchestrated accident. Lingyang had planned to approach the Liondance Troupe in advance. However, what he hadn't accounted for was the fact that rooftops, especially after a bout of rain, proved to be much slipperier than tree branches.
At that point in time, Lingyang, who hadn't yet mastered human language, didn't even possess a name. He was simply referred to as the "little guy" by the members of the Liondance Troupe for a long time.
After futile attempts to communicate with the staff, Lingyang resorted to use a lion head found elsewhere and began a clumsy liondance performance.
Whenever members of the Liondance Troupe recall that performance, they often chuckle and comment, "To be honest, it's more like he was showing off his claws and teeth rather than performing a liondance..."
Fortunately, Lingyang's intention to join the troupe was understood, but his peculiar appearance sparked heated discussions among the members.
In the end, it was the oldest member, addressed as "Master" even by the current leaders of the Liondance Troupe, Mr. Wufang and Mr. Qixing, who made the decision to take Lingyang in.
Lingyang has tried many times to inquire about the reasons for taking him in, but all he gets are vague responses:
"Haha, maybe it's because your ears and tail resemble those of the legendary auspicious lion."
Occasionally, during the master's nap, Lingyang overhears some sleep talk, "'Big Lion'... I fulfilled... the promise to you."
Under the guidance of this master, Lingyang has embarked on a formal journey of learning: delving into lion dancing, calligraphy, and learning how to interact with human beings.
To help Lingyang control the brutal force within his body, Lingyang was taught a set of ancient steps from Huanglong, believed to regulate energy flow and serve as the foundation for lion dancing. With diligent practice, Lingyang officially earned the title of "Liondancer."
In Jinzhou, the term "Lion Head" carries multiple meanings. For ordinary people, it can refer to the "lion head" used in a liondance or the lead performer. While for the liondancers themselves, it refers to a shared dream—the aspiration to become the "head of lions" through diligent practice and perfected lion dancing skills.
"I'm born with this strength and have honed my hunting skills in the wild. However, lion dancing is more than just pure strength—posture, steps, rhythm, coordination with teammates... It's not something that can be learned overnight. My master emphasizes how lion dancing means a lot to Jinzhou people, and I can't help but worry that I might not be meeting their expectations. To live up to the title "Lion Head", I know I've got to put in more work."
In the eyes of his master, Lingyang isn't just talented. More importantly, he possesses an insatiable desire for progress.
Each new move learned is practiced until mastery, and every session of liondance practice continues until the sky is adorned with brilliant stars.
Perched atop the Plum Blossom Pole in the Central Plains, Lingyang gazes his reflection in the water, as if contemplating or challenging his own limits.
Admist falling blossoms, the young Lion Head dances passionately on the poles, which has become a unique spectable in the Central Plains. A Helpful Lion
When it comes to the Central Plains, one can't overlook the annual winter training that the Liondance Troupe diligently undergoes.
The winter months mark the off-season for the troupe, allowing them to gather for intensive training at the grounds in Peach Haven. This thorough preparation is all in anticipation of the upcoming "New Year Parade," where Liondancers from the Jinzhou Theater take to the streets, filling every corner with a vibrant and lively atmosphere. The parade symbolizes bidding farewell to the old year and ushering in the new, making it one of the most significant events in Jinzhou.
As a result, despite being the off-season, members of the Liondance Troupe don't experience the leisure one might imagine. The time is crucial for refining characters, honing skills, crafting props, and rehearsing new performances. However, since they leave the city to the wilderness, Lingyang embraces his true nature.
Even in the winter chill, Lingyang's inherent liveliness keeps him active, much like the fierce beasts inhabiting the nearby mountains. As the world is blanketed in snow, the beasts eagerly embark on their anticipated hunts.
The distant growls of these fierce creatures and the faint cries for help don't escape Lingyang's keen ears. Without hesitation, Lingyang springs into action during breaks, rushing to the woods to deal with the threats. "What was the land at the end of Huanglong like?" Even the wisest researcher cannot provide an answer to this question.
Only the storytellers who pass down tales will describe it as a place once enveloped in dense forests, teeming with life, towering trees, and the prototype of the auspicious beast worshipped by people.
However, after the Lament cast its shadow over the world, the once vibrant land transformed into a Tacet Field, with frozen soil and a haunting silence—a place where not a single creature managed to endure.
At least, this is how Lingyang remembers it since he can remember.
Lingyang can't pinpoint the day he gained memories, but he knows nothing of what happened before that. He doesn't recall his birth, his origins, or even where his journey is leading. At that time, Lingyang, surviving the destruction of his homeland, wandered alone in the wilderness for a long time. Each day was a battle against solitude, extreme cold, and desolation. Lingyang believed his fate mirrored that of all wild beasts—driven by the instinct to survive, wasting his life without discovering a purpose until his last day. Everything changed when he stumbled upon a human military troop.
In the forest during a hunt, Lingyang encountered these peculiar upright animals. He cautiously followed behind them, keenly observing their every move.
Lingyang couldn't help but feel a mix of curiosity and amazement.
Even in the face of formidable enemies, they refused to yield and flee in desperation.
Even when heavily injured, they stood before their wounded comrades, offering protection.
Even in their own hunger, they shared vital supplies with comrades in need.
Lingyang wondered why these upright-walking creatures consistently exhibited behaviors he couldn't comprehend.
However, somehow their "strange" behaviors ignited a small flame within Lingyang's heart, thawing some of the fear and coldness that gripped him.
At that time, Lingyang couldn't fathom why those figures remained vivid in his mind after years had passed, nor could he understand why he decided to follow their scent and footprints, eventually arriving at an unfamiliar distant land—Jinzhou.
Standing on a cliff, Lingyang hesitated, gazing at the lively habitat of these strange creatures in the distance. Suddenly, thunderous gongs and drums echoed, initially startling him. Lingyang thought it was a massive explosion until he witnessed more of these creatures gathering at the city gate.
A dance filled with vibrant energy and genuine happiness unfolded before Lingyang's eyes. In that moment, he finally grasped what he had been seeking all along.
A sense of familiarity washed over Lingyang as he saw the giant lion head leading the dance, a heartwarming connection seemingly rooted in his bloodline.
